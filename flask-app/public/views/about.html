About the Project:

We are a team of Olin College undergraduates students enrolled in Investigating Normal, which reexamines the medical and social paradigms of disability. This webapp is our culminating assignment, a data-driven illustration of the role disability plays in the school-to-prison pipeline. Over the eight weeks, we were advised by Fathom, an information design firm based in Cambridge, MA.

This project aims to weave the data with its context in an accessible way, to give you the opportunity to decide for yourself to what extent disability plays a role in the school-to-prison pipeline. As you navigate our webapp, you can explore large data sets showing suspension data across disability status, race, and geography.

More about our course, Investigating Normal:

Taught by Sara Hendren, Investigating Normal looks at medical as well as cultural tools that investigate the “normal” body and mind. Through readings, site visits, guest speakers, and projects, we investigate both traditional and unusual prosthetics and assistive technologies. We talk to end-users, engineers, industrial designers, artists, and any whose technologies assist with visible and invisible needs, externalize hidden dynamics, and create capacities beyond ordinary functionality. From our acquired context, we then design our own devices — high-tech, low-tech, digital or analog.

By no means was this solely a software development project. From the beginning, we aimed to put the human face of our data front and center. Yet without hard numbers, untangling the messy societal factors affecting disabled minority students is well nigh impossible. After diving into politics, anthropology, and sociology, we realized even we can’t understand the whole picture.
