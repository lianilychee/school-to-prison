Context What the Experts Say

Marginalization multiplies, and we all contribute

“Multiple marginalization is a... narrative to explain how people can be marginalized by different factors...Those marginalizations can compound, and not just compound one plus one” - David

"When a students of color or a black student is sent to the office, they’re often perceived as a troublemaker, so they get suspended or expelled. When white students are sent to the office, they’re perceived as a troublemaker because they’re bored...maybe the material isn’t as engaging for them. See how those are two very different perspectives?" -Elyse

"All of us, as people wrestle with [deficit thoughts] in our daily lives….It is...blaming the victim rather looking at the larger barriers or larger structures in place that can hinder a student’s performance." –Elyse

Teachers don’t have resources in underfunded schools.

“[Pockets of low income schools] have higher caseloads with less resources...to address challenging behavior. If I’m a teacher in that district, it’s not because I’m a bad teacher, I don’t have the same resources and experience.”

“If you don’t have the right kind of professional support, you have good people that leave… I am a teacher that left. It wasn’t about the students, it was about the policy that failed me.” Kristen

“You have really good people leaving because they’re not successful, or you have people that stay and…retire on the job. You have emotionally checked out. I don’t know which is worse.” Kristen

“I'm working in a school right now, a very affluent area, where in every classroom, there is one teacher and...at least one pair professional...That is a resource in and of itself. –Elyse” Kristen

Disabled students are often excluded instead of supported.

“Students with disabilities,...when they’re having issues communicating, its just perceived as behavior problems. If we look at it from a different perspective, maybe it should really be our responsibility to engage those students in different ways. I’m not suggesting it’s easy, but that’s one of those things that ought to be done. -Elyse

“I thought that [the justice system of the united states] was more about rehabilitation but what it has become is about punishment. Our culture has developed into a punishment culture and so we have also these disproportionate levels of suspension of black children in schools.” –Orpheus

"The first step in the school to prison pipeline is when a teacher perceives a student as disruptive or defiant or having some sort of behavior problem. So the teacher will choose to send that kid out to the office with some kind of exclusionary discipline, suspension, maybe expulsion. –Elyse

Solutions are hard, but exist.

“Parent involvement is one of the most difficult and missing links….Parents have tons of rights, especially with respect to disability, to be very involved.” -Kristen

“People have focused on the war on poverty... very low income, danger inflicted neighborhoods, lower socioeconomic neighborhoods that may have violence going on in them. Nothing has ever worked except one thing in terms of turning around the likelihood that people succeed....integration.” –Orpheus

"Ms. Bentley was a really good teacher at getting students engaged, not sending them to the office, setting high expectations."" -Elyse

"School wide positive behavioral support...looks at building a respective positive school climate at the school level. Policies insure that intervention...is looked at in proactive ways instead of reactive ways."" -Elyse
